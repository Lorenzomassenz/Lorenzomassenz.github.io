CHERNOBYL

The nuclear disaster of April 26, 1986

On April 26, 1986, at 01:23, reactor number 4 at the Chernobyl nuclear power plant exploded during a safety test, causing the worst nuclear disaster in history.

La centrale nucleare

  • Position: Northern Ukraine (then part of the URRS)
  • Reactor type: The plant used RBMK-1000 reactors, which were characterized by a design flaw in the control system and the lack of adequate secondary containment.
  • Power: 1000 MW per reactor (Megawatt thermal).
  • Year of construction: 1977-1983

The plant was located near the town of Pripyat, which was founded specifically in 1970 to house the plant's workers and their families. At the time of the disaster, Pripyat had a population of about 49,000. In 1986, Ukraine was part of the Soviet Union, an authoritarian state where secrecy and control of information were central. After the explosion, authorities delayed communication of the disaster, downplaying its severity and putting millions of people at risk.

The Chernobyl disaster highlighted: The limitations of the Soviet system, incapable of managing complex crises with transparency. An impact on public trust that contributed, in the following years, to the collapse of the USSR (1991). The need for more rigorous international nuclear safety standards.

Disaster Timeline

25/04/1986 - 13:05

Test preparation

Power reduction for safety test begins. Protocol violation: reactor is brought to 1,600 MWth instead of the planned 700-1,000 MWth.

26/04/1986 - 00:28

Start of test

The reactor drops to 30 MWth, developing xenon poisoning (temporary shutdown of a nuclear reactor due to the buildup of nuclear poisons in the reactor core) which makes the core unstable.

26/04/1986 - 01:23:40

Emergency button

The AZ-5 emergency button (called SCRAM) is pressed. It was intended to shut down the reactor by inserting all the control rods, but due to a design flaw in the reactor, these increase the reactivity instead of reducing it.

26/04/1986 - 01:23:45

Explosion

Two consecutive explosions destroy the reactor. The first by steam, the second by combustion of hydrogen and graphite.

27/04/1986 - 14:00

Evacuation

About 36 hours after the explosion, Soviet authorities ordered a total evacuation of the city of Pripyat. Citizens were informed that this was a temporary measure and that they would return soon, but they never returned. About 49,000 people were evacuated in a few hours by bus, leaving behind all personal belongings.

The most affected areas were Belarus, Ukraine and Russia, but also Sweden, Poland, Germany, Italy and other European countries detected high levels of radioactivity.

Impact on health

Health consequences include:

  • Increase in cases of thyroid cancer in children
  • Acute Radiation Sickness Among First Responders
  • Long-term psychological effects
  • Soil and water contamination in large areas

Exclusion Zone

The Chernobyl Exclusion Zone is an area of ​​approximately 2,600 km² surrounding the nuclear power plant, created after the evacuation of local communities. The main functions of the exclusion zone are to limit access to the heavily contaminated area, monitor and study the long-term effects of contamination, and allow for cleanup operations, maintenance of the sarcophagus and safe control of radioactivity.

Pripyat

Ghost town, evacuated on April 27, 1986. It had 49,000 inhabitants, mainly plant workers and families.

Red Forest

Pine forest area that received the highest doses of radiation. The trees died and turned red-brown.

New Safe Confinement

Structure completed in 2016 to contain the damaged reactor. Replaces the original "sarcophagus" built in 1986.

Nature and fauna

Paradoxically, the absence of humans has allowed nature to thrive. The area is now home to 60 documented mammal species and 120 documented bird species, with wolf populations 7 times denser than in surrounding areas.
